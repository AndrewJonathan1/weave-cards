# Fresh Website Types Brainstorm

Beyond the already-identified categories (architecture portfolios, weddings, vacation rentals, real estate listings, therapist practices, consultant sites, restaurants), here are 40+ additional website types with real demand.

---

## 1. Funeral / Memorial / Tribute Pages

- **What:** A beautiful memorial page for a deceased loved one — photos, biography, guestbook, funeral details, donation links
- **Who pays:** Families, funeral homes on behalf of families
- **Why $500-$5K:** Extreme emotional urgency, needs to be done in 24-48 hours, families will pay anything to honor someone properly. Funeral homes could white-label this as an upsell to every client.
- **Repeatable:** Highly repeatable — same structure every time (bio, gallery, service details, guestbook)

- **Who pays:** The candidate or their campaign fund
- **Why $500-$5K:** Legal requirement to have a web presence for donation links; campaigns are flush with cash and on tight timelines. Every election cycle creates a new crop of first-time candidates who need this yesterday.
- **Repeatable:** Extremely repeatable — bio, platform/issues, endorsements, donate button, volunteer signup, events

## TOP 10 MOST INTERESTING OPPORTUNITIES

These are ranked by the combination of: money potential, clear buyer, urgency/willingness to pay, repeatability, and how underserved the niche is.

### 1. Funeral / Memorial Tribute Pages (#1 and #21)
**Why it's the best opportunity:** 2.8 million deaths per year in the US alone. Every single one is a potential customer. The emotional urgency is unmatched — families will pay $500-$1,500 without blinking, and they need it within 24 hours (perfect for same-day delivery). The structure is identical every time. You could partner with funeral homes who sell it as an upsell to every family they serve. This is a volume business disguised as a bespoke one.

### 2. Local Political Campaign Sites (#2)
**Why it's fascinating:** Every election cycle (every 2 years, but local elections happen constantly) creates thousands of first-time candidates who need a website immediately. They have campaign funds earmarked for this. The structure is completely standardized. They're not price-sensitive — campaigns routinely waste money on worse things. And every candidate needs one. The timing is predictable, the demand is guaranteed, and the product is identical each time.

### 3. Startup Pitch / Investor Landing Pages (#24)
**Why it's a great niche:** Founders raising money are the least price-sensitive buyers you'll ever find. If you're raising $2M, spending $3K on a page that makes you look fundable is a rounding error. The urgency is built in — fundraising rounds move fast. The structure is identical (problem, solution, traction, team, CTA). And there's a social proof / referral loop: VCs see great pitch pages and ask "who made this?"

### 4. Event Venue / Banquet Hall Sites (#39)
**Why it's overlooked:** Venue bookings are $5K-$50K+ each. A single booking pays for the site many times over. Yet many venues have embarrassingly bad websites because the owner is an operations person, not a marketer. The site structure is completely standardized. And there are tens of thousands of venues — banquet halls, barns, estates, conference centers, hotels with event space. Huge addressable market of high-value businesses with bad websites.

### 5. Construction / Trades / Contractor Sites (#17)
**Why it's a goldmine:** There are millions of trade businesses in the US, most with terrible or no websites. A single lead from the site (average job $10K-$50K) makes the investment trivial. Trade business owners have money but zero time or desire to deal with websites. The structure is identical every time. The volume is enormous. And these businesses are actively searching for this service.

### 6. Retreat / Workshop / Intensive Program Sites (#27)
**Why it's smart:** Retreats and workshops are a booming market of solopreneurs (yoga teachers, executive coaches, wellness practitioners) who charge $1K-$10K per attendee and run 2-4 retreats per year. Each retreat needs a new page. That's recurring revenue from the same client. They're visual, emotional sellers who care about aesthetics but can't build it themselves. The structure is always the same.

### 7. Proposal / Pitch Microsites (#47)
**Why it's a sleeper hit:** Agencies and consultancies pitching $100K+ deals would gladly pay $2K-$5K for a custom pitch site that helps close the deal. The ROI is crystal clear. And each agency pitches multiple times per year — this is a retainer-style relationship, not a one-off. The structure is templated but the content is custom. High perceived value because it's tied directly to revenue.

### 8. Franchise Location / Multi-Location Pages (#25)
**Why it scales beautifully:** Each location is a separate sale with identical structure. A franchise with 50 locations is 50 sites at $1K each = $50K deal. The local franchise owner pays (or corporate requires it). You build the template once and pour in content. This is the most operationally efficient model on this entire list — pure scaling economics.

### 9. Conference / Summit / Symposium Sites (#4)
**Why the economics are compelling:** Events have large, pre-allocated marketing budgets. A $100K event will spend $2K-$5K on a site without hesitation. Events happen every year (recurring revenue). The deadline is immovable — the event date creates urgency. Speaker grids, schedules, and sponsor logos are the same structure every time. Professional associations alone run thousands of conferences per year.

### 10. Daycare / Preschool / Childcare Sites (#14)
**Why it's underappreciated:** Parents are the most motivated researchers on the internet. A daycare's website directly determines enrollment. One enrolled child is $12K-$24K/year in revenue. Most daycares have terrible websites because the owner is focused on running the center. The structure is completely standardized. There are over 500,000 childcare businesses in the US. High value, high volume, highly repeatable, badly underserved.

## Patterns Worth Noting

**Urgency-driven sites** (memorials, campaigns, launches, events) command premium pricing because same-day/next-day delivery is genuinely valuable. This is your differentiator — agencies take weeks.

**High-ticket businesses** (venues, contractors, law firms, financial advisors) pay without flinching because the ROI is obvious. One client from the site > cost of the site.

**Volume plays** (funeral homes, franchise locations, trade businesses) are interesting because the individual sale might be small but the addressable market is enormous and the product is identical each time.

**Recurring revenue opportunities** (retreats, seasonal lookbooks, annual conferences, pitch microsites) let you sell to the same client multiple times per year.

The biggest insight: the most profitable niches aren't the ones with the biggest per-site price tag. They're the ones where the structure is most repeatable, the buyer's urgency is highest, and the ROI is most obvious to the buyer.
