Two Tracks

The same core muscle, two containers. Start with the jewel — simpler, proven. Let the wedding track be shaped by deeper research.

Track One — Start Here

The Wish-Fulfilling Jewel

  • Who it's for
    The person with an unclear felt sense who just wants someone to handle it. Not the DIY Squarespace person. Not the Shopify daily-manager.
  • Who it's not for
    Anyone who needs e-commerce, complex backends, user auth.
  • What happens
    Get on a call, extract their implying, build the thing live or near-live, deliver.
  • What they get
    A static (or Airtable-backed) site that's live and theirs. Custom domain. Works on every device. Done.
  • Proven by
    Nabil — architecture portfolio, built in 15 minutes from one conversation.
  • Warm leads
    Eva, Facebook friend, anyone who's been stuck for months or years.
Track Two — Deeper Research

Wedding Track

  • The shape
    Always 12 pieces. Same thing each time. Repeatability = high value to low effort.
  • PRESENTATION & PROCESS
    Stationery + matching website + AI guest chat + RSVP handling.
  • Killer feature
    The “matching” — nobody offers stationery that matches the website. AI chat where guests ask “where do I stay?” instead of texting the bride.
  • Distribution
    D2C + Service People (wedding planners).
  • Market
    ~2M weddings/year in US. Growing desire for custom over Zola/Knot templates.
  • Channel play
    Venue sites → refers couples → wedding stationery/website service.

The sequencing matters: start with the wish-fulfilling jewel because it’s simpler, proven (Nabil), and warm leads exist. Every client teaches something about the container. Let it be shaped by real encounters, not by planning. Insights flow back to the wedding track.

What's Decided & What's Open

Decided

  • Core service = midwifing coherence Not a website business. Sitting with someone, feeling their world, making it real.
  • Start with wish-fulfilling jewel, not weddings Simpler, proven (Nabil), warm leads exist. Let it be shaped by real encounters.
  • No e-commerce, no complex backends, no Shopify people Complexity kills speed. Speed is the moat.
  • Static + Airtable-backed sites AI-producible, near-zero maintenance.
  • AI features are a genuine technical moat Chat-against-site, intake screening, smart portfolio.
  • Recording-to-AI pipeline = long-term moat Record every conversation. Don't build the AI version yet. Product is you, human, on a call.
  • Simple pricing One price to build, one to maintain, one to change. Three numbers.

Open

  • Which niches to prioritize beyond the first few? Real estate, event venues, therapists all have strong structural fit. Which to pursue first?
  • Exact pricing $1,000 too low? $1,500 founding customer? $3K–$5K normal? Needs real encounters to calibrate.
  • Wedding track timing When to go deeper? After how many general clients? What research still needed?
  • The AI-enabling angle “AI first” felt sense — really enabling clients, not just building for them. What does that mean concretely?
  • Trigger-based vs. relationship-based positioning “We deliver custom overnight” vs. “we do beautiful custom work.” Different positioning, different clients.
  • Scale model Stay artisanal (you on every call) or build toward AI replication? When does the transition happen?
  • Memorial pages — pursue or not? Extreme urgency + emotional depth fits the sensing skill. But low price point and heavy emotional labor.
