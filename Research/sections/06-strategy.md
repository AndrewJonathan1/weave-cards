### Two Tracks

**Track 1 — Wish-Fulfilling Jewel** (now)
Take clients who come to you. Proven model (Nabil). Warm leads exist (Eva, Facebook friend). Revenue from day one. Every client teaches you about the container. Let it be shaped by real encounters, not by planning.

**Track 2 — Weddings** (parallel)
Deeper domain, richer research, higher stakes. The taste extraction tool. The stationery-to-website match. More complex but potentially a monopoly position. Insights flow back to Track 1.

### Distribution

**Primary:** Make it and show it to them. Build first, sell second.

Build someone's site for free on a call. If they want to publish it, they pay. It's that easy.

**Variables that drive conversion:**
- Perceived Value
- Perceived Likelihood of Success
- Trust In Your Character
- Trust in Your Ability

### Pricing Direction

- **Build:** $1,500 founding rate (normally $3K-$5K)
- **Maintain:** $500/year
- **Change:** $500/session

Three numbers. That's it. No dim sum menu, no small/medium/large. Nabil confirmed: itemizing creates hooks for people to argue about.

> If what they need is bigger than a session, tell them honestly — it's a new build at founding-customer rate. That protects from scope creep without categorization overhead.

### The Long-Term Moat

Record every client conversation with consent. Every session is training data for the AI felt-sense extractor. Don't build toward it yet — just record. The product is you, human, on a call. The AI version comes when you have enough pattern to encode.

*Olivia's insight: "Record how you have those conversations and carry forward their implying — just train AI to do that."*
