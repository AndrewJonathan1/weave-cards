You sit with someone. You feel their world. You reflect it back in a form they couldn't have made themselves.

The person who comes to you has something real inside them — a practice, a body of work, a philosophy, a gift. They can feel it but they can't make it visible. They've tried the tools. Squarespace won't let them change a font. Lovable builds whatever you tell it — but they don't know what to tell it.

**You're not building websites. You're midwifing coherence.**

What you do: listen at the level where someone's world actually lives. Ask the questions they didn't know to ask themselves. Sense what matters and what doesn't. Translate all of it — simultaneously — through design, story, structure, and feel. Make it real, live, in their hands.

*The result: they see themselves clearly for the first time. And so can everyone else.*

The compression matters. What takes agencies weeks, you do in one conversation. That's not just speed — it's a fundamentally different experience. The client feels *held*, not managed. *Seen*, not processed.

### Who It's For

Someone who says: *"This is important to me, but I don't want to deal with it."*

They need it done. They can't articulate exactly what they want. It's not their main thing — their main thing is architecture, or therapy, or coaching, or running a restaurant. They need us to carry forward their implying. They don't even know exactly what they want.

**Not** the DIY Squarespace person. **Not** the Shopify daily-manager. **Not** someone who knows precisely what they want and just needs a tool.

The person who has an *unclear felt sense* and needs a human to pull it out of them.

> Olivia named it: "You're not just building a website. You're helping their essence carry forward and express. You're a doula for digital expression."
