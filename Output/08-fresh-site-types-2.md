# Fresh Site Types: 50+ Website Types People Would Pay $500-$5,000 to Have Built

**Excluded from this list** (already identified): Architecture/design firm portfolios, wedding websites, Airbnb/vacation rental sites, real estate per-property listing pages, therapist/coach/healer sites, independent consultant sites, restaurant sites.

## The Full List

### Professional Services & Practices

**1. Law firm or solo attorney site**
- What: Practice area pages, attorney bios, case results, contact/intake form
- Who pays: Solo practitioners or small firms (2-10 attorneys)
- Why $500-$5,000: Credibility is everything. A cheap-looking site loses clients who are about to spend $10K-$100K on legal services. They bill $300-$800/hr — one new client from the site pays for it many times over
- Repeatable: Very. Same structure every time — practice areas, about, results, contact

**2. Dental or medical practice site**
- What: Services, provider bios, insurance info, new patient forms/links, location/hours
- Who pays: Dentists, dermatologists, orthopedists, concierge physicians, specialty clinics
- Why $500-$5,000: New practice openings need credibility fast. Established practices with embarrassing old sites lose patients to competitors. A single new patient is worth $2K-$20K lifetime value
- Repeatable: Extremely. The shape is almost identical across specialties

**3. Veterinary clinic site**
- What: Services, team bios, emergency info, patient portal links, pet care resources
- Who pays: Independent vet practices (not corporate chains)
- Why $500-$5,000: Pet owners choose vets partly on web presence. Independent vets compete against slick corporate chains (Banfield, VCA) with bigger budgets. One new regular client = $1K-$3K/year
- Repeatable: Yes — very similar to medical practice sites

**4. Financial advisor / wealth management site**
- What: Services, philosophy, team, compliance-friendly content, client portal links
- Who pays: Independent RIAs, CFPs, wealth managers
- Why $500-$5,000: Trust and credibility are the entire game. Their clients are high-net-worth — the site needs to feel like money. SEC/FINRA compliance means they can't just slap something up. One new client = $5K-$50K/year in fees
- Repeatable: Yes — same bones, different philosophy and positioning

**5. Accounting / CPA firm site**
- What: Services (tax, audit, advisory), team, industries served, client portal
- Who pays: Small to mid-size CPA firms, solo CPAs with growing practices
- Why $500-$5,000: Tax season is cyclical urgency — they need to look professional when prospects are Googling "CPA near me" in February. One new business client = $3K-$20K/year
- Repeatable: Very

**6. Private tutor / test prep specialist site**
- What: Subjects, credentials, approach, testimonials, scheduling
- Who pays: High-end tutors charging $100-$500/hr for SAT/ACT/GMAT prep or academic subjects
- Why $500-$5,000: Parents spending $5K-$20K on tutoring want to see credentials and professionalism. The tutor's site IS their sales funnel. One new student pays for the site
- Repeatable: Yes

## Top 10 Most Interesting Opportunities

These are ranked by the combination of: money on the table, clear buyer, repeatable format, underserved market, and natural fit with same-day/next-day AI-built delivery.

### 1. Memorial / Tribute Sites (#44)
**Why this is gold:** Emotional urgency creates willingness to pay NOW. Families in grief don't want to learn Squarespace. They want to tell you about their loved one and have something beautiful exist tomorrow. The same-day delivery capability is perfectly matched to the need — funerals are in 3-5 days. The emotional depth of the conversation maps perfectly to the "listening and carrying forward" model. This is one where the AI chat feature could be powerful — guests could ask the memorial site questions and it surfaces the right stories and photos. Price tolerance is high ($2K-$5K) and the buyer (often an adult child with means) doesn't comparison-shop. Very repeatable structure. And there is no incumbent — obituary sites are ugly, funeral homes offer bare-bones pages, and nobody is offering same-day custom memorial sites.

### 2. Conference / Summit / Retreat Sites (#13)
**Why this is gold:** Hard deadline creates urgency (the event has a date). Organizers are too busy planning the event to also build a website. Very repeatable format (speakers, schedule, venue, register). Often annual = repeat client. Price range $2K-$5K fits well. The Airtable-backed dynamic content is a killer feature here — speaker lineup changes, schedule shifts, sponsors get added. Conference organizers are a tight network — one good site gets referrals. And every conference needs a new site each year.

### 3. General Contractor / Construction Portfolio Sites (#35)
**Why this is gold:** Massive underserved market. There are hundreds of thousands of contractors in the US, and their websites are notoriously terrible. They're making $200K-$1M+/year and losing jobs because their website looks like it was built in 2003. They have zero interest in managing a website themselves. The before/after project gallery is a natural fit for portfolio structure you've already built. They know other contractors — referral network is strong. The price ($2K-$4K) is trivial relative to their project sizes ($50K-$500K). This could become a repeatable niche you own.

### 4. Nonprofit Annual Report Pages (#56)
**Why this is gold:** Annual = built-in repeat revenue from the same clients. The format is extremely consistent (impact numbers, stories, financials, donor thank-yous, future vision). Nonprofits with real budgets ($1M+ operating) spend $5K-$15K on printed annual reports — a $2K-$3K interactive web version is an easy sell, especially as a complement or replacement. The AI features could shine here — a donor could chat with the report to find specific program outcomes. Executive directors and development directors are networked — one good report circulates.

### 5. Fundraiser / Gala Event Pages (#14)
**Why this is gold:** Hard deadline. Nonprofits, schools, and hospitals hold galas that raise $50K-$500K. The event page drives ticket sales and sponsorships. A $2K site that increases attendance by 10% is a 10x+ ROI. The buyer is the development team or event committee, and they have budget. Extremely repeatable format. Many organizations hold annual galas = repeat clients. And the urgency is real — event dates don't move.

### 6. Senior Living Facility Sites (#52)
**Why this is gold:** Deeply underserved market with high customer lifetime value ($36K-$120K per resident per year). Adult children researching care options for aging parents are in a high-stress, high-stakes decision moment — they need warmth, clarity, and trust from the website. Most senior living sites are either corporate-bland or dated. Independent facilities don't have marketing teams. The AI chat feature would be extraordinary here — "Does your facility accept Medicaid?" "What's your staff-to-resident ratio?" "Can my mom bring her cat?" Answering these questions via AI chat would be genuinely useful and differentiated.

### 7. Private School / Micro-School Sites (#17)
**Why this is gold:** Tuition is $10K-$50K/year per student. Parents research schools online obsessively. Philosophy-driven schools (Montessori, Waldorf, forest schools, micro-schools) especially need sites that convey their ethos — which maps perfectly to the "carrying forward the philosophy" capability. One additional enrollment more than pays for the site. The micro-school movement is growing rapidly and these are often started by educators who are great at teaching but terrible at marketing. Tight community — school founders know other school founders.

### 8. Pet Breeder Sites (#47)
**Why this is gold:** Non-obvious category with surprisingly high revenue and clear need. Reputable breeders sell puppies for $2K-$5K each and have litters of 5-8. The site signals legitimacy — buyers are actively looking for signs that distinguish responsible breeders from puppy mills. Health testing documentation, parent dog profiles, waitlist applications — it's a well-defined format. Breeders are often in breed-specific communities where referrals flow fast. And most breeder sites are genuinely awful — WordPress templates from 2010 with auto-playing music.

### 9. Winery / Brewery / Distillery Sites (#22)
**Why this is gold:** Craft beverage producers care deeply about brand and aesthetic but don't have in-house digital teams. The site drives tasting room visits ($50-$200/visit) and club memberships ($500-$2K/year). There are 11,000+ craft breweries and 11,000+ wineries in the US. Most of their sites are mediocre. They're in tight communities (wine regions, brewery associations) where one good site gets noticed. The Airtable-backed approach works perfectly for rotating tap lists, wine releases, and event calendars. And the "vibe" of the site matters enormously — a brewery site should feel different from a natural wine bar site should feel different from a Napa estate winery site. This is where the listening and carrying forward really shines.

### 10. Tattoo Artist Portfolio Sites (#48)
**Why this is gold:** Very non-obvious. Tattoo artists making $100K-$300K/year are currently managing their entire business through Instagram DMs. A proper portfolio site with style filtering and a booking system transforms their workflow. They're artists who appreciate good design. Their clients spend $500-$5K per piece. The format is consistent (gallery + styles + booking + FAQ). And tattoo artists are deeply networked — one great site spreads through the community fast. ## Patterns Worth Noting

**Where same-day delivery creates the most value:**
- Memorial sites (funeral is in 3 days)
- Conference/event sites (event date is fixed)
- Campaign sites (election is coming)
- Product launch pages (launch date is fixed)
- Startup landing pages (investor meeting is Thursday)

**Where the AI chat feature creates the most differentiation:**
- Senior living facilities (families have urgent specific questions)
- Medical/dental practices (insurance, conditions treated)
- Memorial sites (guests exploring someone's life story)
- Schools (parents with detailed questions)
- Nonprofits (donors wanting impact specifics)

**Where Airtable-backed dynamic content is the killer feature:**
- Conferences (changing speaker lineups, schedules)
- Wineries/breweries (rotating selections, events)
- Real estate agents (active listings)
- Podcasts (episode feed)
- Contractor portfolios (adding new projects)

**Highest repeatable-structure categories (same bones every time):**
- Medical/dental practices
- Law firms
- Churches
- Real estate agents
- Contractor portfolios
- Conference sites
- Campaign sites

**Networks where one client leads to many:**
- Contractors know contractors
- Breeders are in breed communities
- Wineries are in wine regions
- School founders know school founders
- Nonprofit development directors are deeply networked
- Tattoo artists are in artist communities
