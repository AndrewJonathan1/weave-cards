### 1. M&A ADVISORY / DEAL PROCESS MANAGEMENT

**The complete process:**
- Confidential Information Memorandum (CIM) creation
- Data room setup and organization (hundreds of documents)
- Buyer/investor outreach list building and tracking
- NDA management and tracking
- Financial model creation and scenario analysis
- LOI comparison and scoring
- Due diligence checklist and coordination
- Management presentation deck
- Closing checklist and document coordination
- Post-close integration planning

**Current spending (fragmented):**
- Investment bankers charge 3-5% of deal value (min $150K-$500K retainers)
- Separate data room software ($5K-$25K/deal — Intralinks, Datasite)
- CIM design firms ($10K-$30K)
- Financial modeling consultants ($5K-$20K)
- Legal coordination (billed hourly, $50K-$200K)
- Project management tools (Notion, Asana, spreadsheets — never coherent)

**Where the money comes from:** Business owners selling companies ($1M-$100M deal size). There are ~30,000 lower-middle-market M&A transactions/year in the US. Also: the advisors themselves would pay to be more efficient.

**The coherent AI version:** One conversation with the business owner/advisor → AI generates the CIM draft, organizes the data room structure with document requests, builds the financial model template from their financials, creates the buyer outreach list with scoring criteria, generates all tracking dashboards, and produces the management presentation. All interconnected. All pulling from the same source data. Updated globally when one number changes.

**Why this hasn't been built:** The people who understand deal processes are bankers making $500K+/year who don't build software. The people who build software don't understand the nuance of how a CIM tells a story that connects to the financial model that connects to the buyer targeting.

**Addressable market:** $2B-$5B/year in the US alone (30,000 deals x $100K average addressable spend per deal). Even capturing advisory efficiency tools: $500M+.

**Our price point:** $15K-$50K per deal engagement. Massive value vs. current costs.
